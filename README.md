# 🧠 Psychology Study Guide — Understanding People

A free-first, science-backed study guide for learning psychology with a focus on understanding communication, emotions, and interpersonal behavior. Built as an interactive React app with dark/light mode.

**[→ View the Live Guide](https://mrigank590.github.io/psych-guide)**

---

## What this is

This started as a personal study guide and grew into a structured resource covering:

- Free university courses (Yale, MIT, OpenLearn)
- Vetted books — both free/library and worth-buying
- Key psychological frameworks (attachment theory, love languages, Gottman, CBT)
- Active communities and channels for ongoing learning
- A 12-week suggested roadmap
- A red flags section covering what **not** to waste time or money on

The guide is opinionated on purpose. A lot of content marketed as "psychology for understanding people" is pseudoscience, stereotyping, or outright manipulation content. This guide filters that out and points to what actually holds up under research.

### The roadmap

A 12-week suggested sequence — foundation → emotions → communication → body language → behavior → EQ frameworks → ongoing practice. Designed to be adjusted freely; depth over speed.

### Red flags

Books and content types flagged with sourced criticism:

- "Decode Their Mind" / manipulation-framed products — no scientific basis
- *Men Are From Mars, Women Are From Venus* — contradicted by modern gender research
- *The Female Brain* (Brizendine) — criticized by NYT, WaPo, and the author herself
- Dark psychology / manipulation content — actively harmful to relationships
- Freud-heavy approaches to gender psychology — discredited since the 1960s
- "Mind reading from body language" overclaims — accuracy is low without training

## A note on the premise

There is no shortcut code to crack how any group of people thinks. Science consistently shows that individuals vary far more within groups than between them. What psychology actually gives you is the ability to understand **individual people** — their emotional states, communication patterns, and needs. That skill applies across every relationship, and it starts with learning to ask *"what is this person feeling right now?"* rather than *"what does this message mean?"*
